# Platforms

Treat everything here as **heuristics, not rules**. Platform mechanics and
"ideal lengths" change often. The principles in `retention.md` and `hooks.md`
outlast any specific number. When your experience contradicts a tip here,
defer to your data.

## Shared fundamentals (all three)

- **Vertical 9:16**, shot and edited full-frame.
- **Captions/subtitles always.** Most people watch on mute, so the first words
  must read as a hook on screen, not just in audio.
- **Hook visually *and* verbally in the first frame.** Assume no audio and no
  patience.
- **Respect safe zones.** Keep text and key action clear of the UI: roughly the
  top ~10% and bottom ~20%, and the right-side action rail.
- **One idea per video.** Split multi-part ideas into multiple posts (and tease
  "part 2" to drive follows).
- **Post volume, then double down.** Reach is variable. Ship often, read what
  lands, and make more of the winners. (See `metrics-honesty.md`.)

## TikTok
- Sweet spot often ~15–34s for tight pieces; longer works only if retention holds.
- **Sound matters.** A trending or fitting sound can lift distribution. Match the
  vibe, don't bolt on a random trend.
- Native text hook on screen in the first second; conversational, fast.
- CTA: saves and "comment a word" tend to outperform "link in bio."

## Instagram Reels
- Similar length sweet spot; slightly more polish-tolerant.
- **Cross-posting tip:** strip competitor watermarks (re-export clean). Visible
  logos from other platforms are deprioritized.
- Carousels are a strong *separate* IG format for saveable education (see
  `formats.md` and `assets/carousel-outline.md`).
- CTA: saves and shares to Stories.

## YouTube Shorts
- Tolerates slightly longer and more "useful/educational" framing.
- Strong title/first-line text; loopability helps.
- Shorts can funnel to long-form. A CTA to "full version on the channel" works
  here where it wouldn't on TikTok.

## How distribution gets decided (community-observed heuristics)

These are patterns creators report, **not published platform rules**. They shift
often and some are folklore. Treat them as directional, optimize for the
underlying signal, and defer to your own analytics over any number here.

- **Completion is the master signal.** On YouTube Shorts it surfaces as
  *Viewed-vs-Swiped-Away* (VVSA), the share who watch rather than swipe; higher
  scales further. Average View Duration (AVD) matters too. Don't chase a specific
  percentage; chase "no reason to swipe."
- **Length follows retention, not a target.** Longer Shorts (up to ~50–60s) can
  earn more total watch time *only if* retention holds across the extra seconds. A
  long video that sags loses to a tight short one, so never pad to hit a length.
- **TikTok tests with a seed group first.** A new post is shown to a small cohort;
  strong completion and repeat-loops trigger wider pushes. The first seconds and
  the loop decide almost everything.
- **Instagram Reels rewards shares and saves.** They trigger reach to
  *non-followers*. Build in a reason to send it to a friend or save it for later.
- **YouTube Shorts is also a search engine.** Shorts surface in search for months,
  so titles, descriptions, and keywords keep earning views long after posting.

### Search / SEO
- **Say your keywords out loud.** TikTok and YouTube index spoken audio.
- **Reinforce them in on-screen text and the description/caption.**

### Framing the subject
- **Eyeline rule:** keep the subject's eyes in the **upper third** of the frame.
  If viewers have to hunt for the focal point, they swipe.

## Quick platform-fit checklist

- [ ] Reads as a hook on mute, in the first frame.
- [ ] Captions present, inside safe zones.
- [ ] Length matches how long the payoff actually needs, no padding.
- [ ] Sound choice fits (especially TikTok).
- [ ] One idea, one CTA.
- [ ] No foreign-platform watermark.
